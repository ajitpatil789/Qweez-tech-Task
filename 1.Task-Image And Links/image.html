The Majestic Big Cat of the Wild

The Beauty of Tigers

The tiger, scientifically known as Panthera tigris, is the largest member of the cat family and is known for its power, grace, and beauty.

Interesting Facts About Tigers


Tigers are the largest wild cats in the world, weighing up to 700 pounds.
They have unique stripes, much like human fingerprints, that make each tiger identifiable. Unlike most cats, tigers are great swimmers and often cool off in waterholes during the heat of the day. They are apex predators and play a crucial role in maintaining the balance of ecosystems. Tigers are unfortunately endangered, with only around 3,900 left in the wild.

Conservation Efforts

Due to habitat loss and poaching, tigers are listed as endangered. Many organizations work tirelessly to protect their habitats and prevent poaching.
